What is fat freezing?

Fat freezing is the process of destroying fat cells by freezing them.

There are two ways that fat cells can be destroyed, either by freezing them or by using heat to kill them.

How fat freezing works.

Fat freezing is a non-surgical procedure that uses cold temperatures to break down fat cells. A gel pad is placed over a specific area of the body, which is then surrounded by a machine that cools the pad to around -7 degrees Celsius.

The freezing process kills the fat cells in the target area, which are then metabolized and excreted from the body over the course of several weeks. This process of freezing and thawing is repeated several times throughout the treatment, which typically lasts 45 minutes to 1 hour.


What does a fat freezing treatment involve?

A non-invasive fat freezing treatment, also known as cryolipolysis, involves using controlled cooling to target and kill subcutaneous fat. This contrasts with liposuction, which involves the use of an anesthetic and surgical instruments to remove subcutaneous fat deposits from underneath the skin.

During treatment, a gel pad is applied to the treatment area, which is then covered with a second pad that provides a cooling sensation similar to a refrigerator. The cooling sensation varies in intensity, with some patients reporting a mild stinging sensation while others describe feeling nothing at all.

The cooling sensation lasts for about 40 minutes, after which time the pad is removed. The patient then leaves the treatment area, returning for another session the next time their schedule allows.
